# Marketing Psychology Skill

> 71 psychological principles organized by category for marketing application.

## When to Use This Skill

Apply when:
- Writing persuasive copy and need a specific technique
- Reviewing messaging for psychological effectiveness
- Justifying copy decisions with behavioral science
- Optimizing landing pages, emails, or ads for conversion
- Teaching or explaining why certain approaches work

## BUYER PSYCHOLOGY (20 Principles)

### 1. Loss Aversion
**Definition:** People feel losses 2x more intensely than equivalent gains.
**Example:** "Don't lose your competitive edge" > "Gain a competitive edge"
**When to use:** Urgency messaging, risk framing, churn prevention

### 2. Anchoring
**Definition:** First number/price sets expectation for all subsequent judgments.
**Example:** "Was $997, now $297" makes $297 feel like a bargain
**When to use:** Pricing pages, discount framing, value comparison

### 3. Scarcity
**Definition:** Limited availability increases perceived value.
**Example:** "Only 3 spots left" or "Offer ends Friday"
**When to use:** Limited-time offers, cohort launches, exclusive access

### 4. Social Proof
**Definition:** People follow the actions of others, especially similar others.
**Example:** "Join 140,000+ builders" or "Trusted by teams at Google, Meta"
**When to use:** Hero sections, testimonials, trust badges

### 5. Authority
**Definition:** People defer to credible experts and recognized sources.
**Example:** "Recommended by Y Combinator" or "Featured in TechCrunch"
**When to use:** Credibility sections, founder bios, press mentions

### 6. Reciprocity
**Definition:** People feel obligated to return favors.
**Example:** Free template, guide, or tool before asking for signup
**When to use:** Lead magnets, free trials, content marketing

### 7. Commitment/Consistency
**Definition:** People prefer to act consistently with past statements/actions.
**Example:** "You said you wanted to ship faster..." or micro-commitments
**When to use:** Onboarding flows, email sequences, behavior reminders

### 8. Liking
**Definition:** People buy from people they like and relate to.
**Example:** Founder stories, relatable struggles, "we've been there"
**When to use:** About pages, personal posts, testimonials with faces

### 9. Unity
**Definition:** Shared identity creates stronger influence than shared preferences.
**Example:** "Fellow founders" vs "People interested in startups"
**When to use:** Community messaging, audience identification

### 10. FOMO (Fear of Missing Out)
**Definition:** Anxiety that others are having rewarding experiences without you.
**Example:** "While you're waiting, your competitors are shipping"
**When to use:** Launch urgency, competitive framing, trend messaging

### 11. Bandwagon Effect
**Definition:** People adopt behaviors that others are already doing.
**Example:** "The #1 choice for solo founders" or trending badges
**When to use:** Popularity indicators, growth metrics, movement framing

### 12. Endowment Effect
**Definition:** People value things more once they own them.
**Example:** Free trial strategy - once they've built, they won't leave
**When to use:** Free trials, "start building" CTAs, ownership language

### 13. Status Quo Bias
**Definition:** People prefer things to stay the same (inertia).
**Example:** Frame change as lower risk than staying the same
**When to use:** Migration messaging, "hidden costs of not changing"

### 14. Paradox of Choice
**Definition:** Too many options leads to decision paralysis.
**Example:** 3 pricing tiers, not 7. Clear recommendations.
**When to use:** Pricing pages, feature selection, plan comparison

### 15. Peak-End Rule
**Definition:** Experience judged by peak moment and ending, not average.
**Example:** Nail onboarding first win + final CTA
**When to use:** Onboarding, demos, email sequences

### 16. Confirmation Bias
**Definition:** People seek information that confirms existing beliefs.
**Example:** "You already know AI is changing everything..."
**When to use:** Lead qualification, audience-specific messaging

### 17. Availability Heuristic
**Definition:** Recent/vivid examples seem more common than they are.
**Example:** Fresh case studies, current news hooks
**When to use:** Social proof timing, trend-jacking content

### 18. Sunk Cost Fallacy
**Definition:** People continue investing due to past investment.
**Example:** "You've already built X, now unlock the rest"
**When to use:** Upgrade messaging, expansion revenue

### 19. Halo Effect
**Definition:** Positive trait in one area affects judgment of others.
**Example:** "From the makers of..." or celebrity/expert endorsement
**When to use:** Brand transfer, founder credibility

### 20. Mere Exposure Effect
**Definition:** Familiarity breeds preference.
**Example:** Consistent presence = trust over time
**When to use:** Retargeting, content frequency, brand consistency

## PERSUASION PRINCIPLES (10 Principles)

### 21. Cialdini's 6 Principles
**Definition:** Reciprocity, Commitment, Social Proof, Authority, Liking, Scarcity
**Example:** Use all 6 on landing page for maximum persuasion
**When to use:** Landing page audit, campaign design

### 22. Ethos, Pathos, Logos
**Definition:** Credibility, Emotion, Logic - the 3 modes of persuasion
**Example:** Authority (ethos), story (pathos), ROI calculator (logos)
**When to use:** Content balance, argument structure

### 23. Monroe's Motivated Sequence
**Definition:** Attention → Need → Satisfaction → Visualization → Action
**Example:** Hook → Problem → Solution → Future state → CTA
**When to use:** Sales pages, pitch decks, demo scripts

### 24. Problem-Agitate-Solve (PAS)
**Definition:** Identify problem, make it hurt, present solution
**Example:** "Stuck in spreadsheets? Your competitors aren't. Base44 fixes this."
**When to use:** Ad copy, email hooks, landing page intros

### 25. Before-After-Bridge (BAB)
**Definition:** Current state → Desired state → How to get there
**Example:** "Before: 6 months to ship. After: 2 weeks. Bridge: Base44."
**When to use:** Transformation stories, testimonials

### 26. AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)
**Definition:** Classic marketing funnel progression
**Example:** Hook → Features → Benefits → CTA
**When to use:** Ad structure, email flow, landing page

### 27. "Even If" Framework
**Definition:** Address objections proactively
**Example:** "Build your app in weeks, even if you can't code"
**When to use:** Headlines, subheads, objection handling

### 28. Reason Why
**Definition:** "Because" increases compliance, even with weak reasons
**Example:** "50% off because we're celebrating 100K builders"
**When to use:** Promotions, unusual offers, requests

### 29. Future Pacing
**Definition:** Help people vividly imagine the desired outcome
**Example:** "Imagine waking up to $5K MRR from your app..."
**When to use:** Sales pages, success stories, CTAs

### 30. Contrast Principle
**Definition:** Comparison affects perception of value
**Example:** Show expensive alternative before your price
**When to use:** Pricing comparison, competitor positioning

## FRAMING EFFECTS (3 Principles)

### 31. Decoy Effect
**Definition:** Third option makes one of two others look better
**Example:** $29/mo, $79/mo, $99/mo makes $79 look best
**When to use:** Pricing tiers, plan comparison

### 32. Contrast Effect
**Definition:** Adjacent options shift perception of each
**Example:** Premium plan makes Pro look affordable
**When to use:** Pricing display, feature comparison

### 33. Framing Effect
**Definition:** Same info, different presentation = different decisions
**Example:** "95% success rate" vs "5% failure rate"
**When to use:** Statistics, outcomes, risk messaging

## PRICING PSYCHOLOGY (4 Principles)

### 34. Charm Pricing
**Definition:** Prices ending in 9 or 7 feel cheaper ($297 vs $300)
**Example:** $29, $97, $297, $997
**When to use:** Most pricing, especially consumer/SMB

### 35. Rule of 100
**Definition:** Under $100 = % off. Over $100 = $ off.
**Example:** "30% off" for $50 item, "$50 off" for $300 item
**When to use:** Discount messaging

### 36. Price Relativity
**Definition:** Price is judged relative to reference points
**Example:** "Less than your daily coffee" or "1/10th the cost of hiring"
**When to use:** Value framing, ROI arguments

### 37. Precise Pricing
**Definition:** Specific numbers feel more researched/fair
**Example:** $287 feels more calculated than $300
**When to use:** B2B pricing, premium positioning

## BEHAVIORAL ECONOMICS (9 Principles)

### 38. BJ Fogg Behavior Model
**Definition:** Behavior = Motivation × Ability × Trigger (B=MAT)
**Example:** High motivation + easy action + clear prompt = conversion
**When to use:** CTA optimization, onboarding, UX design

### 39. Nudge Theory
**Definition:** Choice architecture guides decisions without forcing
**Example:** Pre-selected options, default settings
**When to use:** Form design, pricing defaults, onboarding

### 40. EAST Framework
**Definition:** Make it Easy, Attractive, Social, Timely
**Example:** Simple signup, beautiful UI, social proof, urgency
**When to use:** Conversion optimization, UX audit

### 41. Hyperbolic Discounting
**Definition:** People prefer immediate rewards over larger future ones
**Example:** "Start building in 5 minutes" vs "Save hours over months"
**When to use:** Time-to-value messaging, instant gratification

### 42. Default Effect
**Definition:** Pre-selected options are chosen most often
**Example:** "Pro" plan highlighted as recommended
**When to use:** Pricing pages, settings, onboarding

### 43. Choice Architecture
**Definition:** How options are presented affects decisions
**Example:** Order of features, visual hierarchy, grouping
**When to use:** Pricing pages, feature lists, navigation

### 44. Prospect Theory
**Definition:** People evaluate gains/losses relative to reference point
**Example:** Frame as avoiding loss vs achieving gain
**When to use:** Risk messaging, upgrade prompts

### 45. Mental Accounting
**Definition:** People categorize money differently by source/purpose
**Example:** "Pay for itself in first month" (ROI bucket)
**When to use:** Pricing justification, expense framing

### 46. Temporal Discounting
**Definition:** Future rewards valued less than present ones
**Example:** Emphasize immediate benefits, not just long-term
**When to use:** Feature benefits, quick wins, TTV

## GROWTH HACKING (9 Principles)

### 47. Network Effects
**Definition:** Product becomes more valuable as more people use it
**Example:** Templates, community, integrations
**When to use:** Viral mechanics, community features

### 48. Viral Coefficient
**Definition:** Average referrals per user (K>1 = viral)
**Example:** Built-in sharing, referral rewards
**When to use:** Referral programs, shareable outputs

### 49. Habit Formation
**Definition:** Trigger → Action → Variable Reward → Investment
**Example:** Daily login prompts, progress tracking
**When to use:** Retention, engagement features

### 50. Flywheel Effect
**Definition:** Self-reinforcing growth loop
**Example:** More builders → more templates → more builders
**When to use:** Strategy, growth model design

### 51. Compounding Growth
**Definition:** Small consistent gains multiply over time
**Example:** "1% better daily = 37x in a year"
**When to use:** Long-term value props, consistency messaging

### 52. Activation Metrics
**Definition:** Key actions that predict retention
**Example:** "Users who ship 1 app in week 1 have 80% retention"
**When to use:** Onboarding focus, success metrics

### 53. Product-Led Growth
**Definition:** Product is the primary driver of acquisition/expansion
**Example:** Free tier, viral outputs, in-product sharing
**When to use:** GTM strategy, pricing model

### 54. Aha Moment
**Definition:** The point where users realize core value
**Example:** "First time their app works"
**When to use:** Onboarding optimization, TTV focus

### 55. Referral Psychology
**Definition:** People refer to help others + for social currency
**Example:** "Give $50, Get $50" + "I built this with Base44"
**When to use:** Referral program design, shareable content

## MENTAL MODELS (16 Principles)

### 56. First Principles Thinking
**Definition:** Break down to fundamental truths, rebuild
**Example:** "What if anyone could build software?"
**When to use:** Positioning, category creation, innovation

### 57. Inversion
**Definition:** Think about what to avoid, not just achieve
**Example:** "How would we guarantee failure?" then avoid
**When to use:** Risk mitigation, objection anticipation

### 58. 80/20 Principle (Pareto)
**Definition:** 80% of results from 20% of efforts
**Example:** Focus on 3 features that drive 80% of value
**When to use:** Prioritization, feature messaging

### 59. Occam's Razor
**Definition:** Simplest explanation is usually correct
**Example:** Simple headline > clever headline
**When to use:** Messaging clarity, feature simplification

### 60. Hanlon's Razor
**Definition:** Don't attribute to malice what's explained by mistake
**Example:** User didn't churn from anger—just confusion
**When to use:** Churn analysis, user research

### 61. Circle of Competence
**Definition:** Know what you know and stay in it
**Example:** "We're the best at vibe coding, not enterprise consulting"
**When to use:** Positioning, niche focus

### 62. Second-Order Thinking
**Definition:** "And then what?" - consider consequences of consequences
**Example:** Feature enables X, which enables Y, which changes market
**When to use:** Strategy, positioning, long-term planning

### 63. Map vs Territory
**Definition:** The model is not reality
**Example:** Persona is a model—actual users vary
**When to use:** Research humility, testing emphasis

### 64. Survivorship Bias
**Definition:** We only see successes, not failures
**Example:** Case studies show wins—balance with relatability
**When to use:** Realistic marketing, objection handling

### 65. Regression to Mean
**Definition:** Extreme results tend toward average over time
**Example:** Viral post ≠ sustainable reach
**When to use:** Expectations, growth planning

### 66. Opportunity Cost
**Definition:** What you give up by choosing one option
**Example:** "Every week not shipping = week of revenue lost"
**When to use:** Urgency, cost of inaction

### 67. Asymmetric Risk
**Definition:** Upside far exceeds downside
**Example:** "Free trial—nothing to lose, everything to gain"
**When to use:** Risk reversal, trial CTAs

### 68. Compounding Knowledge
**Definition:** Learning builds on learning
**Example:** "Each app you build teaches you for the next"
**When to use:** Platform value, long-term benefits

### 69. Leverage
**Definition:** Small input, large output
**Example:** "One app, unlimited users"
**When to use:** Value props, scale messaging

### 70. Margin of Safety
**Definition:** Buffer for errors and unknowns
**Example:** "Even if half your features fail, you're still ahead"
**When to use:** Risk framing, expectation setting

### 71. Local vs Global Optima
**Definition:** Best local choice may not be best overall
**Example:** "Fixing symptoms vs fixing cause"
**When to use:** Differentiation, strategic positioning
