# Anti-AI Writing Patterns Research

**Source:** Tom Orbach - "The Anti-AI Writing Cheat Sheet" (Feb 26, 2026)
**Purpose:** Incorporate into plugin's brand voice rules and content quality enforcement

## 13 Core Anti-AI Patterns

### Pattern 1: AI uses the same words every time
- **Banned verbs:** leverage, utilize, delve, craft, garner, elevate, amplify, spearhead, streamline, curate, harness, cultivate, navigate, facilitate, embark, empower, bolster
- **Banned adjectives:** groundbreaking, cutting-edge, innovative, robust, seamless, scalable, transformative, unprecedented, dynamic, holistic, comprehensive, compelling, remarkable, pivotal, crucial, meticulous, multifaceted, profound, vibrant, vital
- **Banned adverbs:** drastically, genuinely, remarkably, significantly, strategically, substantially, profoundly, meticulously, notably, truly
- **Banned abstract nouns:** paradigm, ecosystem, landscape, synergy, framework, catalyst, cornerstone, deep dive, game-changer, optimization, transformation, intersection, tapestry, interplay, intricacies

### Pattern 2: AI is obsessed with the number 3
- Groups everything in threes (adjectives, bullet points, parallel phrases)
- Rule: if you spot a trio, cut to two or expand. Two is almost always enough.

### Pattern 3: The "it's not X, it's Y" sentence
- All contrast framing banned: "It's not about X. It's about Y."
- Including escalation: "It's not A. It's not even B. It's actually C."
- Just say what you mean directly.

### Pattern 4: Em dashes everywhere
- AI loves em dashes (â€”). Replace with commas, periods, or parentheses.

### Pattern 5: Dramatic short sentences stacked
- "They launched. They failed. They learned. They grew."
- Sounds like a movie trailer. Break them up, vary rhythm.

### Pattern 6: AI narrates its own writing
- Before points: "Here's why this matters." "The key takeaway is."
- After points: "This highlights..." "This underscores..."
- Between points: "The kicker?" "The bottom line?"
- Delete narration, add specific details instead.

### Pattern 7: "Nobody tells you this"
- "Nobody talks about this." "Nobody tells you this secret."
- Avoid "nobody" as dramatic opener â€” feels manipulative.

### Pattern 8: Giving everything a fake name
- "The Growth Paradox." "The 5-Step Framework." "The SMART Method."
- If it doesn't have a real name, don't capitalize or prefix with "The".

### Pattern 10: Same transition words
- Banned openers: However, Moreover, Furthermore, Additionally, Nevertheless, Notably, Indeed, Consequently, Accordingly, Fundamentally, Essentially, Inherently, Particularly

### Pattern 11: Significance inflation
- "Marking a pivotal moment..." "Setting the stage for..." "A testament to..."
- If it matters, say why with specifics. Don't announce importance.

## Content Patterns to Remove
- **Significance inflation:** "marking a pivotal moment" â†’ explain why with specifics
- **Promotional tone:** "nestled in," "vibrant," "rich cultural heritage" â†’ concrete facts
- **Vague attribution:** "experts say" â†’ name the source or cut the claim
- **Formulaic endings:** "Despite challenges, continues to thrive" â†’ specific fact
- **Generic positive conclusions:** "The future looks bright" â†’ real fact

## Language Patterns to Remove
- **Copula avoidance:** Use "is" and "has" instead of fancy substitutes
- **-ing phrase padding:** Remove participial phrases added for fake depth
- **Contrast framing:** All "it's not X, it's Y" variants banned
- **Synonym cycling:** Pick one term and stick with it
- **False ranges:** Remove "from X to Y" where X/Y aren't a real spectrum
- **Rule-of-three:** Use two when two is enough
- **Fake naming:** Don't capitalize ordinary ideas

## Style Patterns to Remove
- **Boldface abuse:** Don't mechanically bold key terms
- **Vertical lists with inline headers:** Use normal sentences
- **Title case:** Use sentence case for headings
- **Curly quotes:** Use straight quotes
