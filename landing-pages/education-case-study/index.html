The Problem
Hackathons used to end with slide decks. Now they end with live products.

Figma mockups go nowhere

Students spend 24 hours designing screens that never become real products. The hackathon ends, the Figma link goes stale, and the idea dies.

Technical barriers block creativity

Non-technical students have ideas but can't build. Technical students spend all their time on boilerplate instead of the actual product.

No bridge from classroom to market

Traditional curricula teach theory. Students graduate with portfolios of mockups but zero shipped products. The gap between "learned to build" and "actually built" stays wide.
