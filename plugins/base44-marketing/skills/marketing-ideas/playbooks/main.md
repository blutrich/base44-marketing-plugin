## Leads & Conversions (12 Tactics)

### 1. The Waitlist Upgrade
Turn signup into curiosity machine. Show position (#4,521), let them jump line by sharing.
**Best for:** Product launches, beta releases

### 2. The Curiosity Gap Headline
Headlines that create information gaps. "The pricing mistake costing you 23% of revenue" beats "How to optimize pricing."
**Best for:** Blog posts, emails, landing pages

### 3. Baby Reindeer Emails
Cold emails that look like accidents. "Fwd: quick thought" - appears casual, like internal email.
**Best for:** B2B outreach, partnerships

### 4. The Brute-Force Gift
Create something valuable FOR prospect (audit, mockup) before asking for meeting.
**Best for:** High-value prospects, enterprise sales

### 5. Hidden Coupons
Easter eggs in unexpected places - 404 page, footer, support docs. Discovery = sharing.
**Best for:** E-commerce, SaaS with trials

### 6. Decoy Pricing
Add third tier that makes target tier obvious choice.
**Best for:** Pricing pages, subscriptions

### 7. The Reverse Landing Page
Show result before pitch. Lead with output, outcome, transformation.
**Best for:** Tools, services with visible outcomes

### 8. The Calculator Lead Magnet
Interactive calculator quantifying their problem. "How much are you losing to [X]?"
**Best for:** B2B, financial, productivity tools

### 9. The Product Hunt DM Protocol
8 weeks of genuine PH engagement before launch. Reciprocity = guaranteed support.
**Best for:** Product launches, developer tools

### 10. The Phone Hotline Demo
Make demo callable. The call IS the demo. 5% conversion from billboard.
**Best for:** Voice AI, phone services

### 11. The Micro-Commitment Funnel
Quiz → Result → Email → Free tool → Paid. Each yes makes next easier.
**Best for:** Complex purchases, high-consideration products

### 12. The Exit Intent Pivot
Leaving? Offer something different. "Not ready? Get our free guide."
**Best for:** E-commerce, SaaS

## Buzz & Virality (15 Tactics)

### 13. The Impossible Feature
Announce ridiculous feature to spark debate. "Transcription in 47 languages including Klingon"
**Best for:** Feature launches, tech products

### 14. The Accidental Email
"Oops, wrong list - but since you're here..." Curiosity through apparent mistakes.
**Best for:** Product teasers, announcements

### 15. Newsjacking
When big news breaks, be FIRST with commentary. Speed > perfection.
**Best for:** Blog posts, social, newsletters

### 16. The Mini-Game Launch
Wordle-style puzzles, interactive reveals. Play is irresistible.
**Best for:** Consumer products, developer tools

### 17. The Fake Job Title Blast
Everyone changes LinkedIn titles for launch. 50 people = 50 notifications.
**Best for:** Product launches, company announcements

### 18. The Billboard Email (Loops Strategy)
Screenshot email newsletter, buy cheap billboard. "As seen in your inbox."
**Best for:** Email products, newsletters

### 19. Word of Sight
Design for screenshots. Spotify Wrapped, GitHub graphs.
**Best for:** Consumer apps, data products

### 20. The Visible Labor Post
Show work, not just result. Messy middle, iterations, failures.
**Best for:** Founders, creators, products with stories

### 21. The Chicken Game
"Don't click unless you're ready." Reverse psychology works.
**Best for:** Content marketing, social, ads

### 22. Event Hijacking
Show up near events without sponsorship. Counter-party > $1M booth.
**Best for:** Industry events, conferences

### 23. The Niche Landing Page
"The first CRM for left-handed dentists." Absurd specificity = virality.
**Best for:** Horizontal products, SaaS

### 24. The Controversy Stake
Take position competitors won't. Polarization creates loyalty.
**Best for:** Brand building, differentiation

### 25. The Launch Day Countdown
Public countdown with progress updates. Deadline = urgency.
**Best for:** Major launches, events

### 26. The Response Time Flex
"Average reply: 4 minutes." Make speed a feature.
**Best for:** Service businesses, support products

### 27. The UGC Trigger
Design moments for users to capture and share.
**Best for:** Consumer products, apps with outputs

## Customer Sharing (8 Tactics)

### 28. The Status Symbol
Make using product a flex. Limited badges, exclusive markers.
**Best for:** Premium products, communities

### 29. The Customer Spotlight
Feature customers prominently. Make them look successful.
**Best for:** B2B, communities

### 30. The Shareable Result
Design outputs to be screenshot-worthy with subtle branding.
**Best for:** Tools with outputs, analytics

### 31. The Progress Celebration
"You've completed 100 tasks!" with shareable graphics.
**Best for:** Apps with progress, learning platforms

### 32. The Referral Upgrade
"Give your friend 50% off" > "Get $10 for referrals." Lead with giving.
**Best for:** Any referral program

### 33. The Team Feature
Build for groups, market to individuals. Product grows through use.
**Best for:** Collaboration tools

### 34. The Success Template
Pre-written sharing templates. "I just achieved X using Y!"
**Best for:** Products with achievements

### 35. The Advocate Program
Formalize superfans. Exclusive access, direct line to founders.
**Best for:** Products with passionate users

## Competitive Edge (6 Tactics)

### 36. Google Dorking for Leads
`"[competitor] sucks" site:reddit.com` - Find dissatisfied customers.
**Best for:** B2B, SaaS

### 37. Ad Library Intelligence
Facebook Ad Library - longest running = working. Steal what works.
**Best for:** Paid marketing, creative

### 38. The Comparison Page
Create "[You] vs [Competitor]" before others do. Own the SEO.
**Best for:** Products with direct competitors

### 39. Category Creation
Don't claim best X - create new category where you're only player.
**Best for:** Innovative products, crowded markets

### 40. The Feature Parity Decoy
Prominently feature what competitors lack.
**Best for:** Product marketing, landing pages

### 41. The Anti-Positioning
Define by what you're NOT. "Unlike [approach], we believe..."
**Best for:** Brand positioning, manifesto

## Retention & Engagement (8 Tactics)

### 42. The Surprise Upgrade
Randomly upgrade customers. Unexpected generosity = loyalty.
**Best for:** SaaS, subscriptions

### 43. The Win-Back Sequence
Personalized recovery, not generic blasts.
**Best for:** Subscription products

### 44. The Milestone Gift
Celebrate customer milestones - messages, badges, physical gifts.
**Best for:** Products with trackable usage

### 45. The Community Loop
Let customers help each other. Community = switching costs.
**Best for:** Products with learning curves

### 46. The Feedback Feature
Public roadmap with voting. Build what they ask, credit requesters.
**Best for:** Product-led companies

### 47. The Re-Engagement Hook
Weekly digests, new data, fresh challenges.
**Best for:** Apps with ongoing usage

### 48. The Power User Track
Advanced path for best users. Make power user status aspirational.
**Best for:** Products with depth

### 49. The Exit Interview
Genuine exit interviews. Understand, fix, save some accounts.
**Best for:** Subscription products

## Events & Physical (5 Tactics)

### 50. The Anti-Booth Booth
Couches, coffee, games - anything but brochures.
**Best for:** Trade shows, conferences

### 51. The Hallway Hustle
Real event is between sessions. Strategic positioning.
**Best for:** Industry events, networking

### 52. The Pre-Event List
Get attendee lists, reach out first. Pre-warmed > cold.
**Best for:** Industry events

### 53. The Counter-Event
Host your own nearby. Dinners > booths for relationships.
**Best for:** Any industry event

### 54. The Physical Pattern Interrupt
Unusual swag, unexpected experiences. Physical = shareable.
**Best for:** Events, physical retail

### 60. The Physical Product Anchor

### 61. The Live Experience

## Reddit Marketing (4 Tactics)

### 71. The Reddit Loophole
Post RESULTS where brand "happens to be visible." Income screenshots, transformations.
**Best for:** Products with visible results

### 72. The Reddit Native Ad Format
TIFU/AITAH format for ads. Confessions get clicks.
**Best for:** Any product with relatable problem story

### 73. The Stealth Brand Flex
Create shareable moments users WANT to screenshot.
**Best for:** Fintech, fitness, productivity

### 74. The Platform Early Mover
Move fast on new ad platforms. Early = cheap.
**Best for:** Paid acquisition

## Crisis & Competitor Response (3 Tactics)

### 75. Crisis Communication
Own it, fix it, prove it. Short sentences, real responsibility.
**Best for:** Any crisis, outage, mistake

### 76. The Competitor Crisis Hero
When competitor fails, HELP their customers. Don't sell.
**Best for:** Competitive situations

### 77. The Anti-Opportunistic Play
Sometimes silence beats sleaze. Restraint = confidence.
**Best for:** Severe competitor crises
