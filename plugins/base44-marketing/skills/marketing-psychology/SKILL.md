## Quick Reference by Use Case

### Writing Headlines
- Loss Aversion (#1) - frame as avoiding loss
- Anchoring (#2) - set high reference first
- Social Proof (#4) - "Join 140,000+"
- "Even If" Framework (#27) - handle objections in the hook

### Pricing Pages
- Anchoring (#2) - show expensive option first
- Decoy Effect (#31) - 3 tiers, middle looks best
- Paradox of Choice (#14) - max 3-4 options
- Charm Pricing (#34) - end in 7 or 9

### Email Sequences
- Commitment/Consistency (#7) - reference past actions
- Reciprocity (#6) - give value before asking
- Future Pacing (#29) - paint the outcome
- Peak-End Rule (#15) - nail the close

### Landing Pages
- All 6 Cialdini Principles (#21)
- PAS/BAB Framework (#24, #25)
- Contrast Principle (#30) - show alternative cost
- Default Effect (#42) - pre-select recommended

### Objection Handling
- "Even If" Framework (#27) - proactive
- Asymmetric Risk (#67) - "nothing to lose"
- Status Quo Bias (#13) - cost of not changing
- Reason Why (#28) - "because" increases compliance

### Urgency/Scarcity
- Scarcity (#3) - limited availability
- FOMO (#10) - competitors are moving
- Loss Aversion (#1) - what they'll miss
- Temporal Discounting (#46) - immediate reward
